# Self Liquidating Offer (SLO)

The SLO (Self Liquidating Offer) is a strategy focused on converting leads directly into customers (instead of just lead capture). By having the lead purchase an initial low-priced product, the CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) is paid for immediately.

## Context
The primary goal of this funnel **is not to profit on the first sale**, but rather to build a customer base at near-zero or even positive cost (Break-Even Day 0), allowing you to profit later through upsells or LTV (Lifetime Value).

## SLO Workflow (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Entry Product (Front-End)
- **Price:** Very low (e.g., $7, $27).
- **Format:** Something very tangible (Practical E-book, Template, Script, Mini-course).
- **Promise:** Resolves an extremely specific and painful point for the audience.
- **Traffic (Ads):** Advertisement drawing attention to the main pain point and offering the material at a very low price.

### Step 2: Order Bump
- The "Order Bump" appears at checkout right above the buy button. It is crucial for the SLO.
- **Offer:** A natural complement to the entry product, offered at a similar or slightly higher price (e.g., if the ebook costs $7, a $17 masterclass bump).
- **Copy:** Should be a sentence or paragraph highlighting the value and creating a sense of urgency or ease.

### Step 3: "One Time Offer" Upsell (OTO)
- Occurs **immediately after** the payment is approved.
- **Product:** The main product (Core Offer). More expensive (e.g., $97, $297).
- **Pitch:** "Your purchase is almost complete, but before accessing [Product 1], don't miss this unique opportunity for [Product 2]."
- If the customer declines (Downsell), offer a simpler version, a different payment plan, or a discount (e.g., from $97 to $67).

### Step 4: Thank You Page and Delivery
- Thank them for the purchase and detail exactly how they will access all purchased items (Front, Bump, OTO).
- **Reminder:** Insert a button for the VIP group or support contact.
